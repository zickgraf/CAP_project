  [1X1.1 [33X[0;0YIntroduction[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YLet  [23XG[123X be a finite group and let [23XG[123X-mod be a skeletal version of the monoidal
  category  of finite dimensional complex representations of [23XG[123X. The purpose of
  these GAP methods is the computation of the associators of [23XG[123X-mod.[133X
  
  
  [1X1.2 [33X[0;0YQuickstart[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YThe  following  commands  compute  the  associator of [23XD_8[123X and write all data
  necessary  for  the  reproducibility  of  the  computation to files with the
  prefix "D8".[133X
  
  [4X[32X  Example  [32X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27XG := DihedralGroup( 8 );;[127X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27XComputeAssociator( G, true, true, false );;[127X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27Xpath := Filename( DirectoryTemporary( ), "D8" );;[127X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27XWriteAssociatorComputationToFiles( path );[127X[104X
  [4X[32X[104X
  
  
  [1X1.3 [33X[0;0YRead, Write, and Display[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YThe  following  intermediate steps of the associator computation can be read
  from/written to files.[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6YIrreducible  representations of a finite group given by matrices (Data
        1).[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6YDecomposition  isomorphisms  of  tensor  products  into direct sums of
        irreducibles (Data 2).[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YFurthermore, the following data can be written to files.[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6YA database key for the AssociatorsDatabase/DatabaseKeys.g file.[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6YThe final result, namely the associator (Data 3).[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YData 1 and Data 2 involve choices and thus are subject to changes in further
  versions  of  this  package.  However,  the  process  Data  2 -> Data 3 is a
  mathematical   function   and   thus  stable.  For  reproducibility,  it  is
  recommended  to  store  all  three  data.  To  facilitate this task, use the
  function WriteAssociatorComputationToFiles.[133X
